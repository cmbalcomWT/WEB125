Sun Safety

While a bit of sun exposure can be pleasant, excessive exposure can pose risks. Overexposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun can lead to painful sunburns and more severe health issues like skin cancer, premature skin aging, cataracts, eye damage, and weakened immune systems. Children are especially vulnerable. This webpage provides straightforward guidance on shielding yourself and your children from UV overexposure.

Many people are unaware that, despite being largely preventable, skin cancer ranks as the most common form of cancer in the United States, with over 3.5 million new cases diagnosed annually. By adopting simple precautions, you can still relish your time under the sun while safeguarding against overexposure.

The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) suggests practical steps to help you and your family embrace a "SunWise" approach.

  • Do NOT Burn
  • Apply Generous Amounts of Sunscreen
  • Seek Shade
  • Wear Protective Clothing
  • Use Extra Caution Near Water, Snow, and Sand
  • Avoid Sun Tanning and Tanning Beds
  • Get Vitamin D Safely
  • Check the UV Index
